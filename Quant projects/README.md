# Quant Projects

This folder contains Python/Jupyter Notebook work in quantitative finance, market analysis, macroeconomic data, portfolio analysis, risk modeling, and sentiment-based trading.

Some notebooks are connected to coursework, especially discrete-time and continuous-time finance. Others are self-directed projects that apply finance theory to real market data.

## Folder guide

### `Georgia Market Risk/`
Cross-asset research project focused on Georgian-linked equities and regional market risk.

The project studies Georgian-linked equities listed on the London Stock Exchange: Lion Finance Group, formerly Bank of Georgia, ticker BGEO.L; TBC Bank, ticker TBCG.L; and Georgia Capital, ticker CGEO.L.

Live dashboard:

[Georgian Equities Dashboard](https://georgian-equities-dashboard-gmukhigulas1s-projects.vercel.app/)

### `Sentiment Trading Strategy/`
Main research project connected to the bachelor’s thesis, **“The Rise of Reddit in Investment Decisions: A Sentiment-Based Trading Perspective.”**

The project looks at whether Reddit discussion data can be turned into sentiment and engagement signals and tested against market returns. It includes notebooks for data collection, cleaning, ticker mapping, sentiment visualization, weighting, and strategy testing.

### `Portfolio and risk/`
Portfolio and risk-analysis notebooks used to practice return calculation, volatility, diversification, allocation logic, and value-at-risk.

### `Asset Pricing Coursework/`
Coursework and self-study notebooks in mathematical finance, including binomial trees, discrete-time finance, continuous-time finance, and Python implementation of finance concepts.

### `Market Analysis/`
Exploratory stock-market notebooks and small Python scripts. This folder includes work on moving-average backtesting, trendline automation, high/low price-structure logic, simple sentiment strategy testing, and other market-analysis tools.

### `Economics/`
Economics and macro-data exercises. This folder includes notebooks and datasets related to macroeconomic indicators, banking/monetary data, CPI, fiscal data, and world economic outlook-style datasets.
